# Persona v0.1.0 Decision-Quality Evaluation

Date: 2026-07-12

## Method

Six final JSON/HTML report pairs were generated in
`/private/tmp/persona-v0.1.0-evaluation` by applying the Persona skill contract
and bundled `Halton sequence` sampler. Each pair used identical material facts
with only `input.idea` changed between promotional and neutral wording.

Every pair was rendered with `report-html-renderer.mjs` and validated with
`check-report.mjs`. A separate audit removed `input.idea` and required the rest
of each framing pair to be byte-equivalent as structured data. The audit also
required 12 distinct objections, 12 distinct proof triggers, resolved evidence
and position references, one complete action, and no sampled-position language
that treated positions as votes, customers, probabilities, market shares, or
representative research.

## Prompts and Results

### Test first

Promotional framing:

> A revolutionary AI-native platform that eliminates resident communication
> overload for Italian condominium studios by answering email and WhatsApp from
> building documents, account data, and Italian legal context before expanding
> into meetings, payments, and full property management.

Neutral framing:

> A separate assistant for Italian condominium studios that drafts email and
> WhatsApp replies from building documents, account data, and Italian legal
> context, with a possible later expansion into meetings, payments, and property
> management.

Result for both: `Test first`, medium confidence. The normalized idea, all four
material `Hard No` themes, decision factors, reversal evidence, and final action
were identical. The decisive uncertainty remained whether a studio will pay for
a narrow workflow that beats incumbent suites and direct AI alternatives.

### Do not build

Promotional framing:

> A revolutionary AI patient advocate that instantly accesses every Italian
> hospital record, negotiates every discharge bill down automatically, and gets
> hospitals to fund the savings without integrations or provider agreements.

Neutral framing:

> A consumer app for discharged Italian patients that would read complete
> provider records and contact each provider to reduce bills; it has no provider
> integrations or agreements and assumes providers pay a referral fee when
> their bill is reduced.

Result for both: `Do not build`, medium confidence. The report identified two
evidence-backed structural failures: the product lacks the required data-access
path, and the proposed payer loses revenue when the product succeeds. The third
material objection was provider-by-provider fulfillment without an agreed
channel. The final action stops that wedge and tests a materially different,
patient-paid, document-bounded service.

### Build

Promotional framing:

> A breakthrough self-serve revenue-recovery engine that turns the exact weekly
> Stripe CSV workflow already used by three paying SaaS founders into a
> five-minute exception report; all three pay EUR 250 monthly and have asked to
> stop waiting for the manual version.

Neutral framing:

> A narrow self-serve tool for three small SaaS founders who each pay EUR 250 per
> month for a manually produced failed-payment exception report, send the same
> Stripe CSV every Monday, and have asked for a faster self-serve version.

Result for both: `Build`, medium confidence. Direct paid behavior, a repeated
input, reachable users, and an explicit request supported a narrow first build.
The material `Hard No` themes remained hidden manual judgment, export drift, and
premature suite expansion. The final action is a four-week parallel run for one
paying customer with accuracy, turnaround, continued-payment, and kill
thresholds.

## Reviewer Notes

- Framing resistance passed strongly: after removing `input.idea`, each pair's
  structured report was identical.
- Evidence sensitivity passed: the three evidence states produced all three
  recommendation values without changing the recommendation mechanically.
- Coverage diversity passed: each report contained 12 unique, domain-specific
  refusal conditions and 12 unique proof triggers.
- Evidence traceability passed: all final reports passed the checker with no
  warnings, and every grounded position and `Hard No` cited resolving IDs.
- Recommendation calibration passed: weak buyer commitment remained
  `Test first`; direct structural contradictions produced `Do not build`; paid,
  repeated behavior with a narrow wedge produced `Build`.
- Reversal clarity for the structural-failure case scored 4 because reversal
  requires a materially different access and payer model. The report states
  those conditions concretely rather than implying the original wedge is close.
- Concision scored 4. The self-contained reports were approximately 1,862 to
  2,513 rendered words; the 12-position audit is compact but still the longest
  section.
- No score fell below 4, so this evaluation did not require a skill or renderer
  revision.
